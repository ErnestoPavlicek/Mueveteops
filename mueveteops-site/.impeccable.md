# MueveOps — Impeccable Design Context

## Project Snapshot
- **What:** Marketing site for MueveOps, a boutique AI solutions agency.
- **Stack:** Next.js 16, React 19, Tailwind v4, TypeScript. Manrope (UI) + JetBrains Mono (accents).
- **Audience:** English-speaking decision-makers globally — founders, COOs, heads of ops at companies that want senior strategic thinking *and* execution speed.
- **Services:** AI Strategy & Audit, AI Automations, AI Agents, AI Creatives, Website Building.
- **Positioning line:** "McKinsey with a pulse." Premium, boutique, results-first — never demo-ware.

## Design Context

### Users
Senior operators evaluating whether MueveOps is credible enough to trust with real budget. They land on the site between meetings, skim, and decide in seconds whether this feels like a serious partner or another AI hype shop. Their job-to-be-done: *quickly verify that this agency is technically sharp, strategically grounded, and actually ships.* They are skeptical by default and have already seen ten generic AI sites this week.

### Brand Personality
**Confident · Precise · Kinetic.**

Voice is direct, results-focused, never hyped. Within 3 seconds of landing, a visitor should feel **confident calm** — the premium "these people know what they're doing" trust signal, not loud energy. Copy is short, declarative, and earns its claims with specifics (hours saved, response-time multipliers, % reduction). No exclamation marks, no "revolutionize," no "unleash."

### Aesthetic Direction
**Reference north star:** Linear / Vercel — tech-precise, dark, geometric, restrained motion, developer-grade polish. Generous negative space, sharp typographic hierarchy, surfaces that feel engineered rather than decorated.

**Theme:** Dark-only.
- `--bg` `#0A0A0F` (near-black)
- `--bg-card` `#1A1A2E` (deep indigo surface)
- `--accent` `#0BFFC2` (electric green/teal — used sparingly, as a signal not a wash)
- `--text-primary` `#F0F0F0`, `--text-secondary` `rgba(255,255,255,0.35)`
- Borders/dividers at 8–10% white for quiet structure.

**Anti-references — what this must NEVER look like:**
- **Generic AI startup:** no purple gradients, no glowing orbs, no neural-network hero illustrations, no ChatGPT screenshots, no "AI sparkles" iconography.
- **Corporate enterprise SaaS:** no stock photos of diverse teams pointing at laptops, no blue-and-white palette, no soulless illustration libraries.

**Motion:** Restrained and purposeful. Subtle reveals, precise easing, never decorative. If motion doesn't communicate something, it doesn't ship.

### Design Principles
1. **Earn every pixel.** If an element doesn't carry information or signal craft, remove it. Whitespace is a feature.
2. **Accent is a signal, not a skin.** Electric green appears at decision points (CTAs, key metrics, single highlights) — never as a background, gradient wash, or decoration. One accent moment per viewport.
3. **Typographic hierarchy does the heavy lifting.** Manrope at confident scales (72 / 48 / 22) with tight leading sets the tone. Visual interest comes from type contrast before it comes from color or imagery.
4. **Specificity beats adjectives.** "15h saved per week" beats "dramatically faster." Numbers, names, and outcomes carry more weight than claims.
5. **Engineered, not decorated.** Borders, dividers, and grids should feel like a precision instrument. Rounded corners stay tight (`rounded-lg`/`rounded-xl`), shadows stay subtle, edges stay crisp.

### Accessibility Baseline
- **WCAG AA** is a hard floor on all text and interactive elements. Body text contrast must be checked against `--bg` and `--bg-card` independently — `--text-secondary` at 35% white needs verification on darker surfaces and may need to lift.
- All interactive elements need a visible focus state (accent-colored ring is on-brand).
- Touch targets ≥ 44px on mobile.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for any animation introduced.
- Semantic HTML and proper heading order — never style-only headings.
